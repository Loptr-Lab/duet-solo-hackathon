      Fog Mode — Rules

      Terrain

      The board rises from a height of 0 on each side's home rank to a height of 3 across the center two ranks, like the terraced board this mode is modeled on. Terrain affects sight and combat, not movement — pieces still move exactly as in classic chess, including Veiled pieces' one-square restriction.

      Vision & Fog of War

      Each piece sees a radius of squares: 2 for Pawns, 3 for standard pieces, 4 for Rebirth and Death (drops by 1 while Veiled). Standing on higher ground extends that radius by 1 square for every level you're above the target square. Standing on lower ground, you cannot see past a higher square unless it's adjacent to you. Squares are either currently visible, previously seen but stale (shown dimmed — the piece there may have moved since), or unexplored (shown black).

      Querying a square with the command bar (or asking a screen reader to look) reports only what you'd actually know: the true contents if visible, the last-known contents with a staleness warning if stale, or "unexplored" if you've never seen it. You may still attempt a move into fog — legality is checked against the true board, same as a calculated risk in the dark.

      Combat

      Moving onto an enemy-occupied square attacks it instead of capturing instantly. Each piece has HP and an attack value:

      Pawn: 10 HP / 4 dmg · Knight & Bishop: 20 HP / 8 dmg · Rook: 28 HP / 10 dmg · Queen: 40 HP / 14 dmg · Rebirth: 30 HP / 6 dmg · Death: unkillable, cannot attack or be attacked.

      Attacking from higher ground deals 25% more damage. If the target survives, your piece holds its position — the fight continues over future turns. If the target is reduced to 0 HP, it's removed and your piece moves into the square, same as a classic capture (a promoting Pawn becomes a full-HP Rebirth).

      Handoff

      Duet is a single shared screen, so Fog Mode uses an honor-system handoff between turns: pass the device and look away until the next player is ready to reveal their view. This isn't enforced by the software — it works the same way a physical hidden-information board game does.

      Known limits

      The AI opponent and Spectator mode are disabled in Fog Mode — the AI's move selection doesn't yet account for HP combat, so it's human vs. human only for now.

      Lore

      Rebirth

      She was made too bright. Formed by Death after a broken law in Heaven, Rebirth carries a power she hasn't learned to carry safely — anything that lingers too close to her, ally or enemy, gets stripped down to its most basic capability. She isn't cruel. She's simply more than the board was built to hold.

      Death

      Her mentor, and the only stable ground near her. He cannot be captured, cannot be pushed aside, and anything standing close enough to him is shielded from her aura entirely. Not a guardian standing over a weapon — a teacher, still midway through the lesson.

      The Radius of Ruin & Sanctuary

      Rebirth's presence Veils any piece that ends a turn too close to her — reduced to pawn-like movement for a round. Death's Sanctuary is the one place that effect can't reach.
